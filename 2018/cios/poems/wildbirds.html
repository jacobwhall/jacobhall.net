This checkout girl last night
at Wild Birds Unlimited had the same
    first name as you, although
she spelled it kind of differently.

    I read it on the tag pinned
to her lapel, and I remembered how
    you never thought it suited you
and wished you had a different one.

    This girl, she told me to insert my
card because it needed the chip reader—
    I'd swiped instead—I still forget—
she brought me back—I still remember—

    the evening you watched me
struggle to unbutton my flannel shirt,
    wrapped in the calico buzz of
the woods at night, crickets and whip—

    poor-wills, blended sound of
dark and wet and soft with the croon
    of the highway we'd come from
stretched over me like sky, eyes closed.

    Five days until summer and your
fingers still cold from the Yuengling
    you'd opened and continued to sip at
while you went down on me.

    Some stunt. I didn't understand
how I was supposed to react to it.
    You told me how well the other boys
had done, you asked if you could smoke.

    I don't mind. I pulled out with
my bag of birdseed in the same backseat,
    turned on some music to blunt the
racket of my thoughts—nevermind.
