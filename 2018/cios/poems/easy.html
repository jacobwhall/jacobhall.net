It was easy—
    unexpectedly so—
there was nothing to second-guess.
    All I had to do was breathe.

We sat down together
    and watched the far lights of
automobiles crossing the Willamette.
    It was hardly snowing by then.

As the morning commuters
    drove past us, the sun's reflection on
the water tripped towards us,
    yellow as yolk done over easy.

It seemed so close I could
    reach out my hand and touch it
as readily as I could reach over
    and touch you—just as soon.

Nobody talked,
    we felt no need. Memory
reminds you how fast and sparse
    the lucky times are.
