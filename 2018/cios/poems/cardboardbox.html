When we found him lying on the ground
    barely breathing, body still, wings broken
I looked up what we were supposed to do:

The internet said an injured owl should be put
    in a dark and silent place such as
a cardboard box with breathing holes.

I poked out the holes and they wrapped
    him in their flannel. He could only move
his eyes. I put him inside and closed the flaps.

As I did that, I thought of you again
    and I began to worry as I always do,
began to wonder if you were the owl

or if I was the owl. Most likely
    I am the box, since I am made of
cardboard (I don't know if you know this yet)

I tear, I fold, I try again, and if
    you look close you'll see all these
holes poked through my skin.

Last night as I leafed through the books
    on your bedside table, I felt myself fall open:
and all of me was filled so suddenly

by the sharp and vivid sight of us
    hoping and stumbling through the world,
this heart of mine so far so near so flung

by whatever we believe does the flinging.
    I know in the end you are not the owl
and I am not the box, nor is the reverse

true at all. There is no fable to find here,
    just a cardboard box, a dirty flannel,
and a dead bird we will both forget about.
