Eating cold turkey out of a plastic bag
    on a Tuesday night, my thoughts (if
I have any) tend to fall somewhere between
    terror and exhaustion.

Nobody is dead and the stock market
    is doing fine, but still I tremble and
I can't tell why—the reasons must have
    left me, just like you did.

This is the idiom and I am the idiot:
    I hope the night holds you softer,
provides you solace, and lets you sleep
    like you so seldom do.

I hope I learn to see it the right way,
    understanding that some things don't
add up, and it's best not to take
    arithmetic personally.

And I hope you know, however brief
    and however bothersome it is,
however bizarre and uncanny it feels
    to slide into a new mask each year,

none of it is regrettable. Nobody is entirely
    lost or entirely put together, nobody
can justify everything they'e done, nobody has
    really deserved the oxygen they take up,

and it is fine. Ten years from now it won't matter
    what you choose, forsake, and forget about:
the earth's still spinning, god's still dead—
    everyone is always moving on.
