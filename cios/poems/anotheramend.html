Last night, tenth of May, the angels
    must have been elsewhere. I called
you to speculate about my going
    back home, making another amend, and I hope
you heard me.

Ask after a savior and leave a
    message at the tone, wait for the time
to come around, give me a reason
    to keep my word—let me know—say
you hear me.

If you remember the night I
    introduced you to country music,
driving to Shenandoah, I-81—do you—
    would you—come around, listen,
you'll hear me.

This afternoon, this Tuesday
    entropy empty bank account
kind of afternoon, a revelation
    fought its way into my life:
it stood right there

at the doorway, and it wore your
    old flannel. I will miss you longer
than I will have known you,
    this much is sure. I am more
like a crow than a bluebird.
