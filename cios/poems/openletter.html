If we are now living at the doorstep
of the incandescent:
    blind, dazzled, sojourned, slipping—
knock firmly, ring the bell,
    and I am certain they will let us in.

    Because I know what you are
and what you have brought.
    I know you, yours, hers, and mine,
I have seen the hours fly by
    and watched good things sizzle away

    surrendered to the air—you are not air—
you are not blowing past me.
    You are an open letter to the clouds, you are
where the waterstrider finds its
    step, you are sojourned, you are bluebirds,

    you are spontaneous combustion, blind
faith, the racket of rain
    striking tin, the precious and the undeniable—
so, for the time being, please
    remain indispensable, if only for my sake.
