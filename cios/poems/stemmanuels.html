The spires of St. Emmanuel's unpeel the air,
    exposing the space behind the sky where heaven
might fit. She straightens my tie at the doorway,
    (since I don't know how to do it myself)
and asks if I have seen any interesting birds lately.

There was a vulture on I-81, fifteen minutes
    north of Bristol and halfway through the James
Taylor album we played for the drive up;
    it was dining on the distended corpse
of what looked to be a deer (although I wasn't sure)—

Things pass so quickly—at seventy miles an hour,
    you can hardly get a glance in before you run
on northward (faster still). I remember counting
    pelicans with her, chasing slim sandpipers
on the tightrope walk between wave and dune;

And I remember that heron posed on the dock, gray-eyed
    and swift, like an undercover angel sent to guide us
on the morning we let his ashes wing down
    into the water—one bird sign is best, a dream
of him crosses my mind just like a flock of geese crosses

a cloud, returning northward (faster still) I saw
    a different bird disappear behind the belltower,
scraping the sky yet leaving no scar, turning it
    the color of white lilies at the altar—you've come just in time—
this is what I have seen and what I must show you.
